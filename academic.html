Academic Work

Research and Mentorship

01.-

Establishing a Framework for Cancer Research (Late 80s)

Dr. Ghons initiated a structured framework in the late 80s to promote and guide cancer research efforts in Lebanon. Active member of the institutional ethical committee, fostering a research culture among students, caregivers, and patients.

02.-

Pioneering Research in Fellowship Years:

Published articles in renowned journals like the European Journal of Cancer (EJC) and Cancer, focusing on germ cell tumors and testicular cancer during fellowship years.

03.-

Leadership in Cancer Registries and Guideline Development:

Played a pivotal role in the Lebanese Cancer Registry committee, identifying cancer trends specific to Lebanon. Contributed to the development of regional NCCN guidelines in urology and participated in BGICC breast cancer guidelines.

04.-

Significant Contributions to Clinical Trials:

Actively participated in multicentric international clinical trials since the 90s, with lasting impact on guidelines. Involvement in trials related to adjuvant chemotherapy in lung and breast cancers, Docetaxel, Bevacizumab, Everolimus, Ceritinib, Ribociclib, and FOLFOX study in advanced pancreatic cancer.

05.-

Research in Real-World Evidence and New Technologies:

Worked on real-world evidence studies, including OSSMAR, OPERA in Renal Cell Carcinoma, and TRIPOLI study in triple-negative breast cancer. Contribution to new technologies like Next-Generation Sequencing and research on Vinorelbine and Capecitabine (NavCap trials) in breast cancer.

06.-

Leadership Roles and Mentorship:

Served in leadership positions at Saint Joseph University, Hotel Dieu de France University Hospital, and CMC Hospital in Beirut. Co-founded the Lebanese Cancer Research Group (CRG) and the Regional Arab Medical Conference on Oncology (AMCI). Co-founding member and past President of the Lebanese Society of Medical Oncology (LSMO). Active Board Member in the Arab Medical Association Against Cancer (AMAAC).
